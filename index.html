Steve Jobs

American entrepreneur, business magnate, inventor, and industrial designer.

Jobs showing off the iPhone 4 at the 2010 Worldwide Developers Conference

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs' life:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California, U.S
  • 1960 - The Jobs family moves from San Francisco to Mountain View, a suburban town in Santa Clara county, more famous under the name Silicon Valley
  • 1968 - 13-year-old Steve Jobs calls up Bill Hewlett and gets a summer job at the HP factory
  • 1969 - Steve Jobs meets Steve Wozniak, 5 years older, through a mutual friend. Woz and Steve share a love of electronics, Bob Dylan, and pranks
  • 1972 - Steve and Woz build and illegally sell 'blue boxes' that allow to make phone calls for free
  • 1973 - Steve spends the fall semester at Reed College, Oregon, then drops out. He will stay on campus and attend the classes that interest him for a while, then move to a hippie commune
  • 1976 - Apple Computer Inc. is incorporated by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ron Wayne
  • 1977 - Former Intel executive turned business angel Mike Markkula invests in Apple and hires former colleague Mike Scott as CEO. Woz is forced to leave HP to join Apple full time
  • 1978 - The Apple II becomes the first mass-market personal computer, with impressive sales around the US. Apple becomes a symbol of the personal computing revolution
  • 1979 - Steve Jobs is shown the first working graphical user interface at Xerox PARC
  • 1980 - Apple launches the Apple III, which will prove a disastrous flop
  • 1981 - IBM launches the IBM PC, the biggest threat to Apple's future yet
  • 1983 - Launch of the Lisa computer. The Lisa team later merges with the Mac team under Steve Jobs's leadership
  • 1984 - Macintosh is launched in great fanfare at Apple’s annual shareholder meeting
  • 1985 - Alan Kay first introduces the Pixar team to Steve Jobs
  • 1986 - Jobs buys the computer division of George Lucas' ILM for $10 million and incorporates it as Pixar
  • 1988 - Steve Jobs introduces the NeXT Cube in San Francisco to great critical acclaim, pitching it as a workstation for higher education
  • 1989 - Steve shuts down all of Pixar’s hardware operations
  • 1991 - Steve Jobs fires almost half of Pixar’s staff and takes back all of the employees' stock in an effort to cut costs, as the company is still in the red 5 years after its launch
  • 1993 - NeXT fires 300 employees as it discontinues all its hardware operations and becomes NeXT Software Inc. This is the nadir of Steve's career
  • 1995 - One week after Toy Story is out, Pixar goes public. Steve Jobs's worth rises to $1.5 billion, more than it ever was during his first tenure at Apple
  • 1996 - Apple, which was desperately looking for a modern operating system to buy, eventually buys NeXT for $400 million. Steve Jobs is named "informal adviser" to Apple CEO Gil Amelio
  • 1997 - Steve Jobs introduces Apple's new board of directors and a truce with Microsoft at Macworld Boston
  • 1999 - Introduction of the iMac DVs and of iMovie, the first of Apple's first Digital Hub app
  • 2001 - Apple opens its first Retail Stores in Tysons Corner, Virginia and Glendale, California
  • 2002 - Steve Jobs introduces the first Windows-compatible iPods at Macworld NY
  • 2003 - Steve Jobs unveils the Power Mac G5, the world’s fastest computer, at WWDC
  • 2003 - Steve Jobs is diagnosed with pancreatic cancer, but stubbornly refuses any modern medical treatment for months. He tries alternative diets instead
  • 2005 - Steve Jobs makes a memorable commencement speech at Stanford University. History will remember its closing remarks, Steve's advice to the young students: 'Stay hungry, stay foolish', a quote from the last page of the Whole Earth Catalogue from his youth
  • 2005 - Steve introduces the Motorola ROCKR, an iTunes-compatible cell phone, and the iPod nano
  • 2006 - Apple releases its first living-room product, the iPod hi-fi, discontinued a year and a half later
  • 2008 - The press starts speculating about Steve Jobs's health as he appears very thin to unveil the iPhone 3G with an entry price of $199 on stage at WWDC
  • 2009 - Steve Jobs announces he will not speak at Macworld 2009 because of his health, and takes a six-month medical leave of absence
  • 2009 - Google CEO Eric Schmidt leaves Apple's board because of conflicting interests due to Android
  • 2010 - After months of wild rumors, Steve Jobs unveils iPad, 'the biggest thing Apple's ever done'. The tablet runs the same operating system as iPhone
  • 17 Jan 2011 - Jobs surprises the world by announcing his new medical leave of absence, without any end date
  • 2 Mar 2011 Despite his medical leave, Steve Jobs takes the stage to unveil the new iPad 2
  • 6 Jun 2011 - At his last keynote at WWDC 2011, a freil Steve Jobs unveils Apple's cloud offering, iCloud, the foundation for the next decade of Apple products
  • 7 Jun 2011 - Steve Jobs appears at the Cupertino City Council to unveil Apple's plans for its new 'Spaceship' campus. This is his last public appearance
  • 24 Aug 2011 - Steve Jobs resigns as CEO of Apple, with the words 'I have always said if there ever came a day when I could no longer meet my duties and expectations as Apple's CEO, I would be the first to let you know. Unfortunately, that day has come.' Tim Cook becomes Apple CEO
  • 5 Oct 2011 - Steve Jobs dies at home, surrounded by his family
